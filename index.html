A National Rarity

The Fairy Tern

The Fairy Tern, or **tara iti**, is New Zealand's rarest native bird, a delicate coastal seabird with a distinctive black cap. With an estimated population of **fewer than 40 individuals**, it is critically endangered and found only on a few fragile northern sandspits.

Nesting in Peril

Sandspit Nests

The tara iti's nesting behavior is its greatest vulnerability. They create simple, shallow nestsa mere scrape in the sanddirectly on open beaches and sandspits, primarily in Northland locations like **Waipu, Mangawhai, and Pakiri**. This leaves their eggs and chicks highly exposed to tides, storms, and disturbance.

The Predator Threat

Rats Cats and Stoats

The main threat to eggs and chicks comes from introduced mammalian predators. **Stoats, feral cats, and rats** are adept at finding and destroying ground nests. Conservation efforts heavily rely on intensive predator control programs around the known nesting sites to give the chicks a chance at survival.

How You Can Help

The Conservation

Survival relies on human action. The Department of Conservation (DOC) and local groups use fencing, predator control, and supplementary feeding. You can help by keeping dogs strictly away from nesting areas, driving vehicles only in designated spots, and always reporting sightings to DOC.
